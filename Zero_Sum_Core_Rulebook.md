## CHAPTER 1: The Global Horror of Zero Sum

### 1.1 The Core Truth
*Zero Sum* is a tabletop roleplaying game set in the bleeding edge of 2026. This is a game of systemic horror. There are no monsters; instead, there are algorithmic bank runs, cartel hit-squads using militarized drones, AI-generated blackmail, and extraordinary rendition flights to black sites. The players are outmatched, out-funded, and hunted across a global board.

### 1.3 The Grand Unification: The Paradigm Shift
The chaos of the 2020s is not random. The wars, the AI explosion, the crypto crashes, and the climate disasters are all interlocking gears of a single, coordinated meta-plot known as **The Paradigm Shift**. The ultimate goal of the highest-tier factions is the "Post-State Era": bankrupting national governments through proxy wars and crises so that Algorithmic Corporate Hegemonies can take over. Every single crisis relies on the others to herd the global population into total compliance.

### 1.4 The Rules of the Universe
1. **The Panopticon is Real:** Digital footprints are permanent. Facial recognition, gait analysis, and cell-tower triangulation mean you are always being watched.
2. **The Supply Chain is Blood:** The clean tech of the West is built on the horror of PMC-guarded cobalt mines in the DRC and cyber-cartels in Latin America. The horror is global.
3. **Parallel Construction:** Intelligence agencies will illegally spy on you, then orchestrate a mundane traffic stop to legally arrest you.
4. **The "Zero-Trust" Era:** Due to the AI explosion, video and audio evidence are meaningless to the public. You cannot just "leak" the truth; you must force the world to look at it.

## CHAPTER 2: The Psychological Burn Mechanics (Core Rules)

### 2.1 The D10 System & Difficulty Classes
*Zero Sum* uses a pool of 10-sided dice (D10) to resolve conflicts and operational challenges.
1. **Pool Assembly:** Determine the relevant **Attribute + Skill** total for the action. Gather that number of D10s.
2. **Success Threshold:** Roll the dice pool. Every die that lands on a **6, 7, 8, 9, or 10** counts as a **Success** (50% probability). A roll of a 10 counts as two Successes if the character possesses a relevant **Specialization** in that Skill.
3. **Difficulty Classes (DC):** The Game Master (GM) assigns a Difficulty Class based on the complexity, systemic friction, and level of threat:
   * **DC 1 (Standard):** Routine professional actions performed under pressure. Requires 1 Success.
   * **DC 2 (Challenging):** Complicated maneuvers executed under direct threat or active interference. Requires 2 Successes.
   * **DC 3 (Hard):** Elite operations requiring absolute precision against competent opposition. Requires 3 Successes.
   * **DC 4 (Extreme):** Nearly impossible actions attempting to bypass state-level security or overwhelming structural blockades. Requires 4 Successes.

### 2.2 Cognitive Load
The mind and body can only withstand so much tension before the cracks begin to show. A character’s capacity to absorb immediate psychological shock, panic, and physical fatigue is tracked using their **Cognitive Load**.
* **Load Capacity:** A character's maximum Cognitive Load is equal to their **Endurance × 3**.
* **Gaining Load:** Players intentionally "spend" Cognitive Load to push their luck (e.g., "Take 1 Load to reroll a failed die") or automatically gain 1 Load at the end of a particularly harrowing scene.
* **Fully Depleting Load:** If a character's Cognitive Load reaches its maximum capacity (filled to its limit), they immediately suffer a **Psychological Burn** (see Section 2.4).

### 2.3 Refined Desensitization Levels
When characters face truly horrific scenarios—such as discovering a family member's home has been bugged, witnessing an autonomous drone strike, or viewing a cartel torture video—the shock bypasses temporary load and leaves lasting damage. When a character fails a **Detachment** roll under these circumstances, they suffer a condition called **Desensitization**. 

Desensitization manifests in three progressive levels of severity, applying **Situational Complications** (never permanent attribute penalties, to avoid a death spiral):
* **Mild Desensitization:** Represents initial psychological scarring. It imposes a minor situational complication (e.g., *Hypervigilance*—you cannot reroll 1s on social checks; or *Numbness*—you take 1 extra minute to perform fine-motor tasks).
* **Severe Desensitization:** Represents deep, debilitating psychological damage and inflicts a strict **behavioral restriction** (e.g., *Obsessive Paranoia*—the character cannot sleep in the same location twice; or *Panic Response*—the character must spend their first action in combat seeking cover).
* **Psychological Burn (3rd Fail):** If a character is forced to take a third Desensitization condition, or if they fail a Detachment roll while already possessing two active conditions, they immediately trigger a **Psychological Burn** (see Section 2.4). If a character fails a Detachment roll while their third slot is occupied, their psyche collapses entirely: they are retired permanently as a playable character.

### 2.4 Psychological Burn Mechanics
A **Psychological Burn** represents a catastrophic breaking point in a character's psychological defenses. It is triggered under two conditions:
1. **Load Overflow:** The character's Cognitive Load is fully depleted (filled to capacity).
2. **Desensitization Threshold:** The character accumulates **3 active Desensitizations**.

When a Burn occurs, the player must choose one of the following two devastating options to absorb the psychological collapse:
* **Strain a Tie:** The character heavily damages one of their active **Ties** (contacts, family members, or allies). In their panic or cold detachment, the character alienates them. The Tie requires intense narrative upkeep to fix, otherwise it cannot be used.
* **Mental Scar:** The character develops a clinical psychological disorder from the following list:
  * *Amnesia:* The character loses key memories, erasing a past contact or a professional skill (temporarily reducing a Skill to 0).
  * *Catatonia:* In high-stress situations (such as the beginning of a firefight or a chase), the character must roll Detachment (DC 2) or freeze, becoming unable to take actions for the first round of the scene.
  * *Hallucinations:* The character perceives false threats, ghost signals on their devices, or voices, forcing them to waste resources or make checks against threats that do not exist.

Once a Burn is resolved, the character's Cognitive Load is reset to 0, but the permanent scars remain.

### 2.5 Empathy & Ties Link
Human connection is a finite resource governed by one's psychological stability. 
* **Capacity:** A character's maximum number of active **Ties** is strictly limited to their current **Empathy** attribute.
* **Emotional Withdrawal:** If a character suffers severe Desensitization related to Empathy, their Ties become strained. The character's emotional withdrawal, apathy, or paranoid isolation makes it difficult to maintain these relationships, requiring dedicated downtime to keep them active.

### 2.6 The Heat Level (1-10)
The GM tracks the group's global visibility.
* **1-3 (Monitored):** Passive data scraping.
* **4-6 (Targeted):** Bank accounts frozen, passports flagged, social credit wiped.
* **7-9 (Hunted):** Cartel sicarios or Tier-1 operators dispatched. Blackmail released.
* **10 (Erased):** Extraordinary rendition. A black bag over the head. Game over.

## CHAPTER 3: Character Creation (Stats & Trauma)

To survive in *Zero Sum*, a character's psychology is just as important as their physical capability. 

### 3.1 The Attributes (Rated 1-5)
Characters are built on three dualities, containing six attributes.

**The Mental Duality:**
* **Processing:** Raw logic, coding, data analysis, and mathematical deduction.
* **Intuition:** Gut feeling, reading micro-expressions, sensing an ambush.

**The Physical Duality:**
* **Kinetics:** Speed, stealth, sleight of hand, and driving under fire.
* **Endurance:** Surviving sleep deprivation, resisting physical torture, and carrying heavy gear.

**The Psychological Duality (The Horror Stats):**
* **Empathy:** How well you connect with others. High Empathy grants bonuses to social and persuasion skills but makes you highly vulnerable to Desensitization when you witness atrocities.
* **Detachment:** Cold, calculated sociopathy. High Detachment protects you from Desensitization and Cognitive Load, but drastically lowers your ability to maintain Ties or trust others.

### 3.2 Core Skills (Rated 0-5)
* *Technical:* Cryptography, Signal Interception, Forensics, Sabotage.
* *Social:* Interrogation, Bureaucracy, Tradecraft, Subterfuge.
* *Physical:* Firearms (Penalized heavily), Evasion, Close-Quarters, Athletics.

### 3.3 The Desensitization System
Instead of simple "Health," characters suffer specific Desensitization conditions when confronted by the horrors of the real world (e.g., watching a cartel flaying video, realizing your family is targeted, witnessing a drone strike).
As detailed in Chapter 2, when a character fails a **Detachment** roll in a horrific situation, they gain a condition at either the **Mild** or **Severe** level:
* **Paranoia (Severe):** You cannot sleep in the same place twice (behavioral restriction).
* **Obsession (Severe):** You must pursue the conspiracy, regardless of the danger, checking every lead immediately (behavioral restriction).
* **Apathy (Severe):** The horror has broken you. You withdraw emotionally from the world (you cannot utilize any remaining Ties during this state until treated).

## CHAPTER 5: Combat & Autopsies (Why Violence Fails)

In *Zero Sum*, drawing a weapon is a failure state. 
* **Asymmetric Power:** You have a 9mm handgun. The PMC hunting you has thermal-optic drones, body armor, and legal immunity.
* **The Forensic Nightmare:** Firing a gun leaves gunshot residue, casings, and acoustic signatures picked up by city-wide ShotSpotter microphones. Police response is measured in seconds.
* **Damage:** There are no HP sponges. A single bullet causes a **Severe Wound** (bleeding out, -2 to all physical rolls). Two bullets usually result in a **Critical Wound** (death within minutes).

## CHAPTER 6: Factions & Global Crises

### 6.1 The Factions
* **The Five Eyes & Eastern Panopticons:** The global surveillance state. They control the cameras, the satellites, and the social credit scores.
* **The Cyber-Cartels:** Latin American syndicates that employ hackers alongside sicarios. They will live-stream the torture of your loved ones to force compliance.
* **The PMC Warlords:** Private armies guarding African resource mines. They represent the brutal, unregulated physical violence that powers the first world.
* **The Spyware Merchants:** Middle-Eastern and European corporate intelligence firms selling assassination software to autocrats.
* **The Sovereign Wealth Syndicates:** The trillion-dollar dark money networks hiding in offshore tax havens.

### 6.2 Wars & Existential Cover Operations
Wars in *Zero Sum* are massive laundering machines and R&D labs.
* **Active Warzones (Eastern Europe, Middle East):** Used to test autonomous drones and facial recognition on captive populations.
* **Climate Collapse:** Weaponized via "Disaster Capitalism." PMCs secure fresh water aquifers during engineered crises.
* **Bio-Security (Pandemics):** The threat of viruses is used to enforce mandatory DNA harvesting and digital movement-tracking passports.

## CHAPTER 8: Game Master's Guide: Orchestrating Terror

* **Emphasize the Scale:** The players are ants. Let them feel the crushing weight of a global supply chain turning against them.
* **Use the Real World:** Pull headlines. If a real company gets hacked in 2026, put it in the game.
* **Make it Personal:** The cartels don't attack the player's strongpoint; they attack the player's civilian brother.

## CHAPTER 9: Automated Scenario Generation (ZSCM)

### 9.1 The Zero Sum Consistency Matrix
The ZSCM is a mathematical framework to ensure scenarios maintain the brutal asymmetry of the genre.
* **$E$:** Antagonist Power (1-10)
* **$R$:** Player Starting Resources (1-10)
* **$I$:** Initial Heat Level (1-10)
* **$D$:** Degrees of Separation (1-5)

### 9.2 The Mathematical Theorems
1. **The Asymmetry Theorem:** $E = \min(10, \max(5, R + \lfloor I/2 \rfloor))$. The antagonist must be mathematically superior while respecting the 1-10 bounded scale.
2. **The Shadow Theorem:** $D \ge 3$. The true boss uses proxies. Direct conflict breaks the paranoia.
3. **The Footprint Theorem:** Digital MacGuffins require physical infiltration of locations possessing High Security Tiers.
