## CHAPTER 1: The Global Horror of Zero Sum

### 1.1 The Core Truth
*Zero Sum* is a tabletop roleplaying game set in the bleeding edge of 2026. This is a game of systemic horror. There are no monsters; instead, there are algorithmic bank runs, cartel hit-squads using commercial quadcopters with explosives, AI-generated blackmail, and extraordinary rendition flights to black sites. The players are outmatched, out-funded, and hunted across a global board.

### 1.3 The Grand Unification: The Paradigm Shift
The chaos of the 2020s is not random. The wars, the AI explosion, the crypto crashes, and the climate disasters are all interlocking gears of a single, coordinated meta-plot known as **The Paradigm Shift**. The ultimate goal of the highest-tier factions is the "Post-State Era": bankrupting national governments through proxy wars and crises so that Algorithmic Corporate Hegemonies can take over. Every single crisis relies on the others to herd the global population into total compliance.

### 1.4 The Rules of the Universe
1. **The Panopticon is Real:** Digital footprints are permanent. Facial recognition, gait analysis, and cell-tower triangulation mean you are always being watched.
2. **The Supply Chain is Blood:** The clean tech of the West is built on the horror of PMC-guarded cobalt mines in the DRC and cyber-cartels in Latin America. The horror is global.
3. **Parallel Construction:** Intelligence agencies will illegally spy on you, then orchestrate a mundane traffic stop to legally arrest you.
4. **The "Zero-Trust" Era:** Due to the AI explosion, video and audio evidence are meaningless to the public. You cannot just "leak" the truth; you must force the world to look at it.

## CHAPTER 2: The Psychological Burn Mechanics (Core Rules)

### 2.1 The D10 System & Difficulty Classes
*Zero Sum* uses a pool of 10-sided dice (D10) to resolve conflicts and operational challenges.
1. **Pool Assembly:** Determine the relevant **Attribute + Skill** total for the action. Gather that number of D10s.
2. **Success Threshold:** Roll the dice pool. Every die that lands on a **6, 7, 8, 9, or 10** counts as a **Success** (50% probability). A roll of a 10 counts as two Successes if the character possesses a relevant **Specialization** in that Skill.
3. **Difficulty Classes (DC):** The Game Master (GM) assigns a Difficulty Class based on the complexity, systemic friction, and level of threat:
   * **DC 1 (Standard):** Routine professional actions performed under pressure. Requires 1 Success.
   * **DC 2 (Challenging):** Complicated maneuvers executed under direct threat or active interference. Requires 2 Successes.
   * **DC 3 (Hard):** Elite operations requiring absolute precision against competent opposition. Requires 3 Successes.
   * **DC 4 (Extreme):** Nearly impossible actions attempting to bypass state-level security or overwhelming structural blockades. Requires 4 Successes.

### 2.2 Cognitive Load
The mind and body can only withstand so much tension before the cracks begin to show. A character’s capacity to absorb immediate psychological shock, panic, and physical fatigue is tracked using their **Cognitive Load**.
* **Load Capacity:** A character's maximum Cognitive Load is equal to their **Endurance × 3**.
* **Gaining Load:** Players intentionally "spend" Cognitive Load to push their luck (e.g., "Take 1 Load to reroll a failed die") or automatically gain 1 Load at the end of a particularly harrowing scene.
* **Fully Depleting Load:** If a character's Cognitive Load reaches its maximum capacity (filled to its limit), they immediately suffer a **Psychological Burn** (see Section 2.4).

### 2.3 Refined Desensitization Levels
When characters face truly horrific scenarios—such as discovering a family member's home has been bugged, witnessing an automated turret execution, or viewing a cartel torture video—the shock bypasses temporary load and leaves lasting damage. When a character fails a **Detachment** roll under these circumstances, they suffer a condition called **Desensitization**. 

Desensitization manifests in three progressive levels of severity, applying **Situational Complications** (never permanent attribute penalties, to avoid a death spiral):
* **Mild Desensitization:** Represents initial psychological scarring. It imposes a minor situational complication (e.g., *Hypervigilance*—you cannot reroll 1s on social checks; or *Numbness*—you take 1 extra minute to perform fine-motor tasks).
* **Severe Desensitization:** Represents deep, debilitating psychological damage and inflicts a strict **behavioral restriction** (e.g., *Obsessive Paranoia*—the character cannot sleep in the same location twice; or *Panic Response*—the character must spend their first action in combat seeking cover).
* **Psychological Burn (3rd Fail):** If a character is forced to take a third Desensitization condition, or if they fail a Detachment roll while already possessing two active conditions, they immediately trigger a **Psychological Burn** (see Section 2.4). If a character fails a Detachment roll while their third slot is occupied, their psyche collapses entirely: they are retired permanently as a playable character.

### 2.4 Psychological Burn Mechanics
A **Psychological Burn** represents a catastrophic breaking point in a character's psychological defenses. It is triggered under two conditions:
1. **Load Overflow:** The character's Cognitive Load is fully depleted (filled to capacity).
2. **Desensitization Threshold:** The character accumulates **3 active Desensitizations**.

When a Burn occurs, the player must choose one of the following two devastating options to absorb the psychological collapse:
* **Strain a Tie:** The character heavily damages one of their active **Ties** (contacts, family members, or allies). In their panic or cold detachment, the character alienates them. The Tie requires intense narrative upkeep to fix, otherwise it cannot be used.
* **Mental Scar:** The character develops a clinical psychological disorder from the following list:
  * *Amnesia:* The character loses key memories, erasing a past contact or a professional skill (temporarily reducing a Skill to 0).
  * *Catatonia:* In high-cognitive load situations (such as the beginning of a firefight or a chase), the character must roll Detachment (DC 2) or freeze, becoming unable to take actions for the first round of the scene.
  * *Hallucinations:* The character perceives false threats, ghost signals on their devices, or voices, forcing them to waste resources or make checks against threats that do not exist.

Once a Burn is resolved, the character's Cognitive Load is reset to 0, but the permanent scars remain.

### 2.5 Empathy & Ties Link
Human connection is a finite resource governed by one's psychological stability. 
* **Capacity:** A character's maximum number of active **Ties** is strictly limited to their current **Empathy** attribute.
* **Emotional Withdrawal:** If a character suffers severe Desensitization related to Empathy, their Ties become strained. The character's emotional withdrawal, apathy, or paranoid isolation makes it difficult to maintain these relationships, requiring dedicated downtime to keep them active.

### 2.6 The Heat Level (1-10)
The GM tracks the group's global visibility.
* **1-3 (Monitored):** Passive data scraping.
* **4-6 (Targeted):** Bank accounts frozen, passports flagged, social credit wiped.
* **7-9 (Hunted):** Cartel sicarios or Tier-1 operators dispatched. Blackmail released.
* **10 (Erased):** Extraordinary rendition. A black bag over the head. Game over.

## CHAPTER 3: Character Creation (Stats & Psychological Burn)

To survive in *Zero Sum*, a character's psychology is just as important as their physical capability. 

### 3.1 The Attributes (Rated 1-5)
Characters are built on three dualities, containing six attributes.

**The Mental Duality:**
* **Processing:** Raw logic, coding, data analysis, and mathematical deduction.
* **Intuition:** Gut feeling, reading micro-expressions, sensing an ambush.

**The Physical Duality:**
* **Kinetics:** Speed, stealth, sleight of hand, and driving under fire.
* **Endurance:** Surviving sleep deprivation, resisting physical torture, and carrying heavy gear.

**The Psychological Duality (The Horror Stats):**
* **Empathy:** How well you connect with others. High Empathy grants bonuses to social and persuasion skills but makes you highly vulnerable to Desensitization when you witness atrocities.
* **Detachment:** Cold, calculated sociopathy. High Detachment protects you from Desensitization and Cognitive Load, but drastically lowers your ability to maintain Ties or trust others.

### 3.2 Core Skills (Rated 0-5)
* *Technical:* Cryptography, Signal Interception, Forensics, Sabotage.
* *Social:* Interrogation, Bureaucracy, Tradecraft, Subterfuge.
* *Physical:* Firearms (Penalized heavily), Evasion, Close-Quarters, Athletics.

### 3.3 The Desensitization System
Instead of simple "Health," characters suffer specific Desensitization conditions when confronted by the horrors of the real world (e.g., watching a cartel flaying video, realizing your family is targeted, witnessing an automated execution).
As detailed in Chapter 2, when a character fails a **Detachment** roll in a horrific situation, they gain a condition at either the **Mild** or **Severe** level:
* **Paranoia (Severe):** You cannot sleep in the same place twice (behavioral restriction).
* **Obsession (Severe):** You must pursue the conspiracy, regardless of the danger, checking every lead immediately (behavioral restriction).
* **Apathy (Severe):** The horror has broken you. You withdraw emotionally from the world (you cannot utilize any remaining Ties during this state until treated).

## CHAPTER 5: Combat & Autopsies (Why Violence Fails)

In *Zero Sum*, drawing a weapon is a failure state. 
* **Asymmetric Power:** You have a 9mm handgun. The PMC hunting you has thermal-optic sights, body armor, and legal immunity.
* **The Forensic Nightmare:** Firing a gun leaves gunshot residue, casings, and acoustic signatures picked up by city-wide AcousticNet microphones. Police response is measured in seconds.
* **Damage:** There are no HP sponges. A single bullet causes a **Severe Wound** (bleeding out, -2 to all physical rolls). Two bullets usually result in a **Critical Wound** (death within minutes).

## CHAPTER 9: Automated Scenario Generation (ZSCM)

### 9.1 The Zero Sum Consistency Matrix
The ZSCM is a mathematical framework to ensure scenarios maintain the brutal asymmetry of the genre.
* **$E$:** Antagonist Power (1-10)
* **$R$:** Player Starting Resources (1-10)
* **$I$:** Initial Heat Level (1-10)
* **$D$:** Degrees of Separation (1-5)

### 9.2 The Mathematical Theorems
1. **The Asymmetry Theorem:** $E = \min(10, \max(5, R + \lfloor I/2 \rfloor))$. The antagonist must be mathematically superior while respecting the 1-10 bounded scale.
2. **The Shadow Theorem:** $D \ge 3$. The true boss uses proxies. Direct conflict breaks the paranoia.
3. **The Footprint Theorem:** Digital MacGuffins require physical infiltration of locations possessing High Security Tiers.

## Core Gameplay Loop

At the heart of *Zero Sum* is the D10 Dice Pool system, representing a character's technical proficiency, tactical training, and psychological endurance. To perform an action, players roll a number of D10s equal to their relevant attribute and skill, counting any die that lands on a 6 or higher as a Success. Complex tasks require multiple Successes, and the environment is entirely hostile: combat is lethal, resources are strictly monetized, and every action carries systemic risk. There are no supernatural threats—only the terrifying reality of debt, surveillance, and corporate violence.

The psychological toll of this existence is tracked through **Cognitive Load**, **Desensitization**, and **Psychological Burn**. As players witness atrocities or compromise their morals for survival, their Cognitive Load increases. If unmanaged, this escalating Load hardens into Desensitization, permanently stripping away their humanity and empathy. Reaching critical mass results in Psychological Burn, where the character suffers a complete, debilitating breakdown in the face of the crushing bureaucratic nightmare they are trapped within.

## Metadata Exhaust

Metadata Exhaust complements Cognitive Load perfectly. While Cognitive Load represents *internal* psychological deterioration, Metadata Exhaust represents *external* systemic heat. Every time a player uses a digital tool, engages in combat, or utilizes a Tie, they generate Metadata Exhaust. When a player's Exhaust exceeds their Technical Attribute, the GM automatically gains a "Parallel Construction" token—allowing the GM to suddenly freeze bank accounts, revoke digital access, or deploy a corporate hit squad.

**The Grey-Market Wash (Scrubbing Exhaust):**
To scrub Metadata Exhaust, players must physically interact with the game's Analogue Mechanics during Downtime. A player can hand physical **Analogue Market tokens** (representing untraceable cash or physical favors) to the GM to "pay off" a data scrubber at a 1-to-1 ratio (1 token = 1 Exhaust removed). 

If the player is out of physical tokens, they can still scrub their Exhaust, but they must explicitly write a new, permanent line item in their physical **Debt Ledger** (e.g., "Owe Apex-Sovereign one favor - $50,000 equivalent"). The physical act of writing the debt permanently binds the character to the corporations they are fighting. For every new debt written, they reduce their Metadata Exhaust by 1.


## V2.1 Debriefing Optimization Patch

Based on extensive playtesting and simulated feedback, the following mechanical tweaks have been officially implemented to optimize the *Zero Sum* experience:

### 1. Tactical Cover & Evasion
* **The Issue:** Evasion DCs in combat were punishingly high, rapidly draining ammunition tokens and making survivability too low even for prepared characters.
* **The Fix:** **Tactical Cover Bonus.** If a character spends a narrative action to secure physical cover (e.g., behind concrete, server racks, or an armored vehicle) before an attack, their Evasion DC is automatically reduced by 2. Furthermore, they may spend an Analogue Token to guarantee their cover holds for the duration of the firefight.

### 2. Deep Penetration (Multi-Stage Hacking)
* **The Issue:** Hacking felt too simple and binary, lacking the tension appropriate for our zero-trust setting.
* **The Fix:** Hacking is no longer a single check. Bypassing corporate security or secure servers requires a **Deep Penetration Sequence**. The Hacker must achieve 3 cumulative Successes across multiple turns. However, each failed roll immediately generates +1 Metadata Exhaust, escalating the risk of the system tracing their physical location before they finish the hack.

### 3. Ties & The Narrative Override
* **The Issue:** Players felt that their "Ties" (Contacts) were under-utilized and wanted more narrative ways to escape danger or bypass censorship.
* **The Fix:** **The Narrative Override.** Once per session, a player can permanently "burn" one of their Ties to automatically succeed on any non-combat social, investigative, or evasion check (e.g., smuggling a decrypted drive past a checkpoint). Burning a Tie means that contact is compromised, arrested, or goes permanently off-grid to save the player. 

### 4. Streamlined Debt Ledger
* **The Issue:** Calculating exact monetary equivalents for favors was slowing down the game flow at the table.
* **The Fix:** **The Favor Tier System.** The Debt Ledger no longer requires precise dollar amounts. Favors are now strictly tiered:
  - *Minor Favor* (e.g., passing a security checkpoint, a burner phone): Reduces Exhaust by 1.
  - *Major Favor* (e.g., erased flight records, a weapons cache): Reduces Exhaust by 3.
  - *Soul Bound* (e.g., a corporate hit, full identity scrub): Reduces Exhaust by 5, but the GM gains absolute narrative control over the character's next Downtime phase.

### 5. Instant Cognitive Load
* **The Issue:** The triggers for Cognitive Load calculations were occasionally too ambiguous, leading to table debates.
* **The Fix:** **Instant Triggers.** No calculations needed. A character automatically suffers +1 Cognitive Load if they:
  1. Take any physical damage.
  2. Burn a Tie.
  3. Witness the death of an ally or innocent.
  4. Fill their Metadata Exhaust track.

# Analogue Mechanics: Offline Brutalism

*Zero Sum* is designed to be played entirely offline, utilizing physical pen, paper, and physical tokens to simulate the suffocatingly real horrors of bureaucracy and hyper-capitalism. Digital tools are not required.

## 1. The Physical Debt Ledger
Each player is issued a **Debt Ledger Sheet**. This replaces the traditional "gold/currency" section of a character sheet.
- **Red Ink and Black Ink:** Players are required to track their finances using red and black pens. Positive balances are written in black; debts are written in red.
- **The Ledger as a Burden:** Every time a player buys ammo, pays a bribe, or gets billed for collateral damage, they must physically strike out their previous balance and write the new one. The paper should become messy, stressed, and overwhelming over the course of a campaign.
- **Generational Debt:** When a character dies, the player does *not* throw away their Debt Ledger. They physically cross out the deceased character's name, write their new character's name at the top, and continue adding to the negative balance. 

## 2. The Contract Signing (EULA)
Before any mission or major loan is granted, the GM must provide a physically printed **Standard Independent Contractor Agreement (EULA)**.
- **The Ritual:** The GM slides the printed paper across the table. The players must physically sign the document with a pen.
- **The Fine Print:** GMs are encouraged to hide brutal, mechanical penalties deep within the text (e.g., organ forfeiture, exorbitant interest rates, waiving rights to life-support if injured). If the players don't read it before signing, the GM enforces the penalties mercilessly.

## 3. Physical Resource Tokens
Scarcity is a core theme. Numbers on a page are easily ignored, but physical tokens are not.
- **Tracking:** Use pennies, poker chips, or actual metal casings to represent ammunition, and a different token to represent daily rations.
- **Handing Over Tokens:** When a player fires their weapon, they must physically hand a token back to the GM. When they are out of tokens, they are out of ammo. This creates a tactile, rising panic as players watch their physical pile of survival resources dwindle.

## 4. The Analogue Market Crash Table
![The Black Market Kiosk](assets/black_market_kiosk_redacted.jpg)

Instead of a digital ticker, the GM rolls for Market Fluctuations in front of the players at the start of every session (or whenever they visit a vendor).
- **The Market Roll:** Roll a D10 Dice Pool on the **Market Crash Table**.
- **Outcomes:** A low roll means the market is stable (prices are standard). A high roll indicates a crisis (e.g., Syndicate blockade, Corporate tax hike), applying a percentage multiplier (e.g., +50%, +200%) to all basic goods like ammo and medicine for the duration of the session.

## 5. The Itemized Receipt
At the end of a session, instead of simply awarding "XP and Loot," the GM physically hands each player a printed **Itemized Operational Receipt**.
- The GM fills in the blanks: Gross Payout, Ammo Deductions, Bio-Hazard Cleanup, and Net Profit.
- Players must then copy their Net Profit (which is often a negative number) onto their Debt Ledger in red ink.

### Metadata Exhaust & The Debt Ledger
Players must scrub their Metadata Exhaust using the physical Debt Ledger. By physically writing a new debt line item, a player reduces their Metadata Exhaust by 1. Alternatively, they can hand the GM an Analogue Market token to erase 1 Exhaust.

# Bureaucracy and Law: The Silent War
![The Boardroom](assets/spire_boardroom_redacted.jpg)

In *Zero Sum*, the most dangerous adversaries do not carry firearms. They carry clipboards, injunctions, and itemized billing software. Combat against the system is fought through litigation, metadata scraping, and leveraging secrets.

## 1. The Auditors
![The Auditor](assets/auditor_redacted.jpg)

The Auditors are corporate accountants flanked by heavily armed, private military contractors. They represent the apex predators of the financial ecosystem. 
- **Modus Operandi:** Auditors do not shoot; they arrive mid-firefight or shortly after to serve immediate legal injunctions.
- **Abilities:** They can remotely freeze a character's bank accounts, revoke DRM licenses on medical pacemakers or weaponry, and legally claim a character's internal organs to settle defaulted debts.
- **Engagement:** Engaging an Auditor with violence is financial suicide. Every bullet fired at an Auditor incurs a 1,000% "Aggravated Assault Surcharge" billed directly to your next-of-kin.

## 2. OSINT and Blackmail (Social Combat)
There is no "Charisma" stat. You cannot smile your way out of a corporate execution. Social encounters are resolved using **Leverage**.
- **Mechanic:** Players must use Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT), metadata scraping, and doxxing to find a target's pressure points before negotiations begin.
- **The Cost of Winning:** Winning a negotiation means you successfully leveraged a threat (e.g., exposing a Syndicate boss's offshore accounts). However, to do so, you inevitably leave a digital footprint or expose your own vulnerabilities. Every social victory creates a future noose.
- **Example:** You secure a cache of medical supplies from a broker by threatening their family, but the transaction log permanently records your biometric signature, making you traceable.

## 3. Collateral Damage Billing
Combat is not just physically destructive; it is a financial disaster.
- **Ubiquitous Sensors:** Corporate zones are saturated with micro-sensors that log every missed shot, broken window, and drop of spilled blood.
- **The Itemized Bill:** When resolving combat, players are hit with a literal itemized bill.
  - *Unauthorized Bio-Hazard Cleanup (Per liter of blood spilled): $400*
  - *Municipal Property Damage (Per bullet hole in corporate drywall): $1,200*
  - *Disruption of Commerce Surcharge: $5,000*
- **Resolution:** If the bill exceeds the payout of the gig, the players end the session deeper in debt than they started.

# Campaign Modules: Headlines to Heists

Here are three ready-to-play campaign hooks directly based on the lore and NPCs of the 2022-2026 period. 

## Campaign 1: The Apex Cold Wallet
* **The Premise:** Simon Apex-Sovereign (Sam Bankman-Fried equivalent) is awaiting trial in a secure federal holding facility. But the missing $8 billion from his collapsed crypto exchange, Apex, wasn't lost—it was hidden. 
* **The Hook:** A burned lawyer (Sarah Chen) approaches the players. She has one half of a multi-sig cryptographic key. The other half is in a safe deposit box in the Geneva Freeports.
* **The Twist:** Apex-Sovereign didn't just steal money from investors; he was laundering money for the "Krieger-Vanguard" (Russian PMC). Now, Krieger-Vanguard hitmen, FBI agents, and cartel fixers are all hunting for the keys. The players must break into the Freeports, secure the drive, and figure out how to launder or whistleblow the funds without getting assassinated.

## Campaign 2: The Apex-Sovereign Ledger Shard
* **The Premise:** Jeffrey Apex-Sovereign is dead, but his leverage lives on. The "Apex-Sovereign Ledger," containing the darkest secrets of politicians, royals, and CEOs, was broken into three encrypted shards by his blackmail insurance team.
* **The Hook:** An investigative journalist who got too close to one of the shards is found dead (ruled an "improbable suicide"). The players find their encrypted notes, pointing to a server farm in Ashburn, Virginia.
* **The Twist:** The shard is currently held by The Architect's "Omni-Stat Global" corporation. They aren't turning it over to the authorities; they are using it to feed their AI behavior-prediction models. The players must infiltrate the Ashburn data center, air-gapped from the internet, and extract the data while Omni-Stat Global's private corporate security ("The Auditors") lock down the building.

## Campaign 3: The Deepfake President
* **The Premise:** It's the eve of a major global summit in 2026. President Joseph Omni-Stat Global-Stat Global is scheduled to make a speech that will drastically escalate the proxy war in Eastern Europe.
* **The Hook:** The players are contacted by Jack "Discord" Taylor, an NSA analyst currently on the run. He shows the players irrefutable proof that the President suffered a severe medical emergency three days ago and is completely incapacitated. The man scheduled to give the speech is an incredibly sophisticated AI-generated hologram and voice clone, operated by a shadow cabinet pushing for war.
* **The Twist:** The players have 48 hours to expose the deepfake. However, because of the AI explosion of 2023-2024, no one trusts digital evidence anymore. They can't just leak Jack's files; they have to physically crash the summit in Washington D.C. (heavily guarded by Secret Service and Faction 1 agents) and disrupt the broadcast on live, analog television before the war is declared.

# Clinical Psychosis: Grounding the Paranormal

In *Zero Sum*, the paranormal is not magical; it is a manifestation of severe clinical psychology, environmental poisoning, and systemic cognitive load. The human mind simply breaks under the weight of the Paradigm Shift.

## 1. Mass Psychogenic Illness (MPI)
The entities known as *The Corporate Auditors* are not supernatural demons. In reality, they are regular, minimum-wage human Repo-Men and Riot Police executing corporate mandates.
- **The Delusion:** The unmedicated, collective psychological burn of society—combined with the heavy neurotoxic smog that blankets the Omni-Stat—triggers a shared psychosis (Folie à Deux / MPI).
- **The Visuals:** The human mind refuses to process the reality of their cruelty. As a defense mechanism, it mentally censors these enforcers into terrifying, surreal "Fratzen" (distorted, nightmarish faces). The horror is the brain protecting itself from the mundane reality of human evil.

## 2. Clinical Prosopagnosia (Face-Blindness)
The aesthetic of faceless, shadowy figures is directly tied to the mechanics of psychological burn and empathy collapse.
- **Mechanic:** As a character's Psychological Burn score increases, the empathy and recognition centers of their brain begin to shut down, inducing cognitive load-induced prosopagnosia. 
- **Gameplay Effect:** Characters literally lose the ability to recognize human faces. At high Psychological Burn levels, the GM can force players to roll an Observation check simply to distinguish their own allies from enemies in chaotic situations. Everyone becomes a shadowy, featureless liability. Paranoia becomes mechanical.

## 3. The Fracture Table (Based on the Corporate Psychological Evaluation Matrix (CPEM))

- **Tachycardia & Tremors:** Heart rate explodes, hands shake uncontrollably. *-20 to all precision and combat checks.*

# Zero Sum: A Modern Thriller Roleplaying Game

## Core Concept
*Zero Sum* is a tabletop roleplaying game set in the present day (2026). There are no monsters, no magic, and no futuristic tech to save you. The real horrors are systemic: corrupt corporations, unchecked intelligence agencies, black money networks, and ruthless political machines. 

The players take on the roles of ordinary people—journalists, whistleblowers, forensic accountants, activists, or disgraced professionals—who have stumbled onto a conspiracy far larger than themselves. They have seen something they shouldn't have, and now, powerful entities want them silenced.

## Tone & Atmosphere
* **Perpetual Paranoia:** The protagonists should never feel safe. Their phones might be tapped, their bank accounts can be frozen at any moment, and their loved ones are potential leverage.
* **Asymmetric Conflict:** The enemies have endless resources, legal immunity, and institutional power. The players have their wits, limited resources, and the truth.
* **Lethality:** Combat is not a sport; it's a desperate fight for survival. A single gunshot wound can be fatal or require a trip to an illegal back-alley doctor that compromises their location.
* **Plot Twists:** Trust is a luxury. Allies have ulterior motives, the "good guys" are compromised, and the conspiracy constantly shifts shapes when you think you've figured it out.

## Setting the Stage: 2026
The world is teetering on the edge. Economic instability, deepfakes, AI-driven misinformation campaigns, and extreme political polarization have created a powder keg. Against this backdrop, massive shadow organizations manipulate markets, rig elections, and eliminate dissidents without a trace.


# --- core_mechanics.md ---

# Core Mechanics: The Paranoia Engine

## The System
*Zero Sum* uses a simple D10 dice pool system. You roll a number of 10-sided dice equal to your Attribute + Skill. Any die that rolls a 7 or higher is a Success. Multiple successes determine the magnitude of your achievement.

## The Cognitive Load & Paranoia Tracks
Instead of just tracking physical Health, characters track **Cognitive Load** and **Paranoia**.
* **Cognitive Load:** Gained from physical exhaustion, close calls, and minor setbacks. High cognitive load penalizes rolls.
* **Paranoia:** Gained when characters realize they are being watched, when an ally betrays them, or when they uncover terrifying truths. High paranoia forces characters to make irrational decisions to "stay safe" (e.g., abandoning a safehouse early, destroying perfectly good equipment, cutting ties with contacts).

## The Heat Level
The Game Master (GM) maintains a **Heat Level** for the group (ranging from 1 to 10). 
* **Level 1-3:** Background checks, passive surveillance.
* **Level 4-6:** Active tailing, frozen bank accounts, smear campaigns.
* **Level 7-9:** Wanted by law enforcement, hitmen dispatched, media blackout.
* **Level 10:** Total burn. The full force of the state or corporate security apparatus is hunting you.

## Combat is Failure
If guns are drawn, the players have already made a mistake. Damage is realistic and devastating. There are no "hit points" that magically replenish. Wounds cause bleeding, infection, and severe mechanical penalties.

## The "Twist" Mechanic
Once per session, the GM or the Players can spend a "Burn Point" to introduce a severe twist. A contact turns out to be a double agent; the encrypted drive actually contains a tracking virus; the safehouse is already compromised.


## V2.1 Debriefing Optimization Patch

Based on extensive playtesting and simulated feedback, the following mechanical tweaks have been officially implemented to optimize the *Zero Sum* experience:

### 1. Tactical Cover & Evasion
* **The Issue:** Evasion DCs in combat were punishingly high, rapidly draining ammunition tokens and making survivability too low even for prepared characters.
* **The Fix:** **Tactical Cover Bonus.** If a character spends a narrative action to secure physical cover (e.g., behind concrete, server racks, or an armored vehicle) before an attack, their Evasion DC is automatically reduced by 2. Furthermore, they may spend an Analogue Token to guarantee their cover holds for the duration of the firefight.

### 2. Deep Penetration (Multi-Stage Hacking)
* **The Issue:** Hacking felt too simple and binary, lacking the tension appropriate for our zero-trust setting.
* **The Fix:** Hacking is no longer a single check. Bypassing corporate security or secure servers requires a **Deep Penetration Sequence**. The Hacker must achieve 3 cumulative Successes across multiple turns. However, each failed roll immediately generates +1 Metadata Exhaust, escalating the risk of the system tracing their physical location before they finish the hack.

### 3. Ties & The Narrative Override
* **The Issue:** Players felt that their "Ties" (Contacts) were under-utilized and wanted more narrative ways to escape danger or bypass censorship.
* **The Fix:** **The Narrative Override.** Once per session, a player can permanently "burn" one of their Ties to automatically succeed on any non-combat social, investigative, or evasion check (e.g., smuggling a decrypted drive past a checkpoint). Burning a Tie means that contact is compromised, arrested, or goes permanently off-grid to save the player. 

### 4. Streamlined Debt Ledger
* **The Issue:** Calculating exact monetary equivalents for favors was slowing down the game flow at the table.
* **The Fix:** **The Favor Tier System.** The Debt Ledger no longer requires precise dollar amounts. Favors are now strictly tiered:
  - *Minor Favor* (e.g., passing a security checkpoint, a burner phone): Reduces Exhaust by 1.
  - *Major Favor* (e.g., erased flight records, a weapons cache): Reduces Exhaust by 3.
  - *Soul Bound* (e.g., a corporate hit, full identity scrub): Reduces Exhaust by 5, but the GM gains absolute narrative control over the character's next Downtime phase.

### 5. Instant Cognitive Load
* **The Issue:** The triggers for Cognitive Load calculations were occasionally too ambiguous, leading to table debates.
* **The Fix:** **Instant Triggers.** No calculations needed. A character automatically suffers +1 Cognitive Load if they:
  1. Take any physical damage.
  2. Burn a Tie.
  3. Witness the death of an ally or innocent.
  4. Fill their Metadata Exhaust track.

# Master Audit: Expert Critiques & Proposals

The Hivemind has completed its ruthless evaluation of the *Zero Sum* framework. The consensus is that while the premise is terrifying and highly original, the mechanics and lore suffer from mathematical death spirals, legal vulnerabilities, and "cartoonish" narrative leaps.

Below is the consolidated critique and the proposed roadmap for V2.

## 1. Legal & Copyright (The Liability Pass)
*   **Libel Risk (High):** Using 1:1 real-world figures (Elian Moss, Simon Apex-Sovereign) and assigning them massive felonies is actionable defamation. **Proposed Fix:** Decouple them. Create composite archetypes. Rename "Chirp" and "Omniverse" to distance from real trademarks.
*   **Copyright Proximity:** "Cognitive Load Track" and "Fracture" are legally uncomfortably close to *Alien RPG*. **Proposed Fix:** Rename the mechanics to **"Cognitive Load"** and **"Psychological Burn."**
*   **Sovereign Void Fallacy:** International waters are not lawless; they are governed by flag-state UNCLOS laws. **Proposed Fix:** Reframe the Void as a **"Jurisdictional Labyrinth."** PMCs don't kill you because there are no laws; they kill you because the local magistrate is bought and the ship flies a Bahamian flag of convenience.
*   **Parallel Construction Mechanics:** **Proposed Fix:** Make this an active battleground. If players realize they are being framed via parallel construction, give them legal/social mechanics to expose the "Fruit of the Poisonous Tree" and drop the charges.

## 2. Immersion & World-Building (The Coherence Pass)
*   **The Unplayable Panopticon:** If facial recognition and AI are mathematically flawless, the players will die in session one. **Proposed Fix:** Introduce **"Systemic Rot."** The surveillance state is bloated, siloed, and corrupt. The NSA won't share facial recognition with corporate PMCs due to proprietary disputes. Cameras have blind spots because city councils embezzled the maintenance budgets.
*   **Disaster Capitalism vs. Master Plan:** Claiming the Factions *started* the climate crisis and the Ukraine War is comic-book villainy. **Proposed Fix:** Pivot to pure **Disaster Capitalism**. The factions didn't start the fires, but they ruthlessly profit from them. 
*   **Algorithmic White-Listing:** To explain why PMCs can have shootouts while players get arrested for firing a 9mm, introduce **Cryptographic White-Listing**. PMCs carry tokens that tell city-wide acoustic sensors to ignore their gunfire. Players can steal or spoof these tokens to buy 5 minutes of consequence-free violence.

## 3. PnP Mechanics & Math (The Balance Pass)
*   **Broken Difficulty Classes (DCs):** With successes on a 7-10 (40% chance), a 4-dice character has a mere 2.5% chance of passing a DC 4. This forces elite operators to act like amateurs. **Proposed Fix:** Lower the success threshold to **6+ (50% per die)** or firmly cap DCs at 3.
*   **The Psychological Burn Death Spiral:** Permanently lowering Attributes due to Psychological Burn creates a death spiral where failing a roll permanently cripples your dice pool, guaranteeing more failures. **Proposed Fix:** Traumas should inflict **Situational Complications** (e.g., "Cannot reroll 1s", "Catatonia during ambushes") rather than permanently subtracting dice.
*   **The ZSCM Script is "Fraudulent":** The Asymmetry theorem math currently breaks the 1-10 bounded scale by forcing the Antagonist Power up to 20. The footprint checking uses hard-coded city strings instead of systemic logic. **Proposed Fix:** Refactor the math to respect the 1-10 scale ($E = \min(10, \max(5, R + \lfloor I/2 \rfloor))$). Update the python script to assign and verify dynamic "Security Tiers" rather than parsing for specific city names.

# Hyper-Logistics: Weaponized Economics

In *Zero Sum*, the economy is the deadliest enemy in the game. Combat shouldn't just drain hit points; it should bankrupt you. Logistics are not a background mechanic—they are the primary driver of horror.

![Corporate Debt Notice](assets/corporate_debt_notice_redacted.jpg)

Characters do not start at zero; they start with crippling debt (housing, medical, oxygen taxes). **Debt is a core stat.**
- **The Transfer:** If a character dies or their Psychological Burn maxes out, they aren't simply removed from the game. Due to the "Guarantor Act," their negative balance is legally transferred to their designated emergency contact.
- **Next-of-Kin:** The player's *newly rolled character* is this emergency contact. You inherit your predecessor's financial ruin, ensuring that death offers no escape from the Board's ledgers.

## 2. Algorithmic Surge Pricing
Corporate medical dispensers and ammo kiosks are monitored by advanced biometrics.
- **Surge Mechanic:** If a player is bleeding out and stumbles to a kiosk, the machine’s biometric sensors detect their elevated heart rate, adrenaline, and desperation. The algorithm automatically triggers "Surge Pricing," raising the cost of bandages, coagulants, and stims by up to 400% in real-time. Survival is a luxury priced for the desperate.

## 3. DRM & Subscription Gear
![DRM Locked Weapon](assets/drm_locked_rifle_redacted.jpg)

You do not own your firearm or your water purifier; you license them. 
- **Digital Rights Management:** Weapons feature corporate DRM. If you miss a subscription payment, or if you attempt to shoot a Board executive (violating the Terms of Service), the gun's smart-trigger "bricks" itself via Bluetooth.
- **Jailbreaking:** "Jailbreaking" gear becomes a mandatory, high-risk survival skill. Firing a jailbroken weapon carries a risk of catastrophic malfunction, but it is the only way to ensure the Board cannot remotely disarm you.

## 4. The Gig-Economy of Violence
There are no "quest givers" in the traditional sense. Jobs are dispensed via mandatory, unfeeling algorithmic apps (e.g., *Taskr* or *Hustl*).
- **Blind Bidding:** Players must blindly bid on contracts against other desperate crews. 
- **The Margin:** If they bid too low to secure the job, the payout won't even cover the cost of the ammo and calories expended to complete it. Players are forced to constantly calculate the ROI (Return on Investment) of pulling the trigger.

# Items, Technology, and Logistics

In the modern world, information is a weapon, and your smartphone is a tracking device. Equipment in *Zero Sum* is about survival, staying off the grid, and gathering intelligence.

* **Burner Phones (Pre-paid):** Essential for communication. Must be discarded after a few uses or a specific operation. (Cost: Low. Availability: High).

* **YubiKeys / Hardware Tokens:** Physical security keys required for accessing encrypted drives. If the players lose it, the data is gone forever.

## Cyber Warfare Tools
* **Stingray (IMSI Catcher):** A device the size of a suitcase that mimics a cell phone tower, forcing all nearby phones to connect to it. Used to intercept calls, texts, and track locations. Highly illegal, heavily used by law enforcement and private intelligence.
* **Zero-Click Exploits:** Software that can compromise a target's phone just by sending them a message (they don't even have to click a link). Grants full access to camera, microphone, and encrypted apps. Extremely rare and expensive.
* **Tails OS USB Drives:** An amnesic incognito operating system that boots from a USB stick and routes all traffic through the Tor network, leaving no trace on the host computer.

## Firearms and Violence
Guns are loud, leave forensic evidence, and escalate the Heat Level immediately.
* **Concealed Handguns (e.g., Glock 19, Sig P365):** The most common weapons. Easy to hide, but firing one without a suppressor guarantees a police response within minutes in an urban area.
* **Untraceable "Ghost Guns":** 3D-printed receivers combined with standard parts. No serial numbers. Expensive to acquire on the black market, but vital for deniable operations.
* **Psychological Burn Kits:** Since hospitals are required to report gunshot wounds, players must rely on military-grade psychological burn kits (tourniquets, QuikClot, chest seals) and underground doctors to survive.

## Financial Tools
* **Monero / Privacy Coins:** The only way to move money anonymously. Bitcoin is entirely traceable by forensic accountants.
* **Pre-paid Debit Cards (Straw Purchases):** Bought with cash by a third party (a "straw buyer") to pay for hotels, servers, or travel without linking to the player's identity.

# The Meta-Plot: The Paradigm Shift

In *Zero Sum*, the chaos of the 2020s is not random. It is not the result of competing factions stumbling over each other. It is a highly orchestrated, interlocking mechanism designed to permanently alter the rules of the world. 

This overarching conspiracy is known internally as **The Paradigm Shift** (or "The Post-State Transition").

## The Ultimate Goal: Corporate Feudalism
The true objective of the highest tier of the Factions (The Sovereign Wealth Syndicates and the Tech Titans) is the abolition of the sovereign nation-state. Democratic governments are too slow, too regulated, and too unpredictable. The Shift is a coordinated effort to bankrupt and destabilize national governments so completely that they collapse, leaving Algorithmic Corporate Hegemonies to step in and "save" the population.

In this emerging **Post-State Era**, the concept of citizenship is completely dismantled, replaced by tiered **Corporate Employment Contracts**:
* **Rights as Terms of Service (TOS):** Fundamental rights are replaced by corporate terms. Access to housing, clean water, healthcare, and security are no longer civic guarantees but corporate benefits.
* **The Termination Penalty:** Termination of employment is a civilian death sentence. If a worker is fired, their biometric credentials are deactivated, they are evicted from corporate housing, and their legal status is erased. They are cast out into the unpoliced, toxic wasteland of the "municipal ruins" surrounding the smart cities.


## How Every Crisis is Connected
Everything you have seen over the last four years is a gear turning in this machine. Nothing is isolated.

### 1. The AI Explosion (The Cognitive Primer)
You cannot overthrow governments if the populace can still organize. The generative AI flood (2023-2024) was intentionally unleashed to destroy objective reality. By flooding the internet with deepfakes and algorithmic noise, they created a "Zero-Trust" society. Without a shared reality, mass protests and revolutions are impossible to coordinate.

### 2. The Crypto Crash and Programmable CBDCs (Financial Excommunication)
The collapse of exchanges like Simon Apex-Sovereign's "Apex" was engineered to terrify the public away from decentralized finance. By destroying crypto, the Syndicates forced populations into the arms of Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs). 

Once money is programmable, the Factions can instantly execute **Financial Excommunication** on anyone flagged as a systemic threat:
* **Smart-Contract Isolation:** A dissident's digital currency is programmed with automated restrictions. It can expire if not spent immediately, and it is blocked from purchasing restricted items like transit tickets, burner phones, or encryption keys.
* **Economic Containment:** Any peer-to-peer transaction with a flagged dissident automatically triggers a contagion warning on the sender's account, deducting a compliance fee or freezing their wallet. The dissident is financially isolated, forced to starve in the shadows as their currency becomes useless.


### 3. The Proxy Wars (Distraction & R&D)
The horrific grinding wars in Eastern Europe and the Middle East are not just for territory. They are **The Distraction**. By forcing national governments to pour trillions of dollars into proxy wars, those nations are rapidly marching toward sovereign bankruptcy. Simultaneously, these warzones act as **The R&D Labs** for the automated perimeter turrets and facial recognition tech that will soon be used to police the new corporate mega-cities.

### 4. Climate Collapse (Disaster Capitalism)
The climate crisis wasn't engineered by the Factions, but they are the ones ruthlessly capitalizing on it. As wildfires, heatwaves, and flooding render rural areas uninhabitable, the Factions use supply-chain manipulation to restrict aid. The global population is thus forced to migrate into hyper-dense, corporate-owned "Smart Cities." The disaster is the shepherd; the Panopticon is the pen.

### 5. PMCs and Cyber-Cartels (The Physical Asset Grab)
While national armies are distracted by the proxy wars, who is securing the actual future of the planet? Private Military Companies and Cyber-Cartels are acting as deniable corporate shock troops. They are violently seizing the physical assets required to run the Post-State world: lithium mines in Africa, fresh water aquifers in Latin America, and fiber-optic choke points across the oceans.

## The Tipping Point (2026+)
The game begins at the precipice of The Paradigm Shift. It is not fully sprung; we are in the aggressive **beta-test phase**. The smart cities are experimental zones. CBDCs are rolling out, but cash still lingers in the slums. Factions have intentionally left backdoors in their own CBDC networks to move dark money, giving players a shadow infrastructure to parasitize. The protagonists of *Zero Sum* are the few people who have seen the strings connecting the cartels, the data centers, the warzones, and the stock market. 

They are not just trying to survive; they are trying to stop the world rules from changing forever.

# Rules of the Universe (Operational Logic)

To run *Zero Sum* effectively, players and GMs must understand the fundamental rules of the 2026 modern world. If you break these rules, the system will crush you.

## 1. Digital Footprints are Permanent
* **The Rule:** Everything connected to the internet leaves a log. Every credit card swipe, toll booth pass, facial recognition camera, and cell tower ping is recorded.
* **The Consequence:** You cannot just "delete" a video or "hack the mainframe." To erase data, you must either physically destroy the servers holding the backups or corrupt the data pool so heavily that the truth is indistinguishable from noise.

## 2. Violence Escales Asymmetrically
* **The Rule:** If you shoot someone, the state will bring its full weight down upon you. You cannot out-gun a SWAT team or a private military contractor.
* **The Consequence:** Violence must be the absolute last resort, used only to break contact and escape. If the players engage in a prolonged firefight, they are guaranteed to lose.

## 3. The Power of "Parallel Construction"
* **The Rule:** Intelligence agencies often find out what they want to know using illegal surveillance. To use it in court without revealing their methods, they use "parallel construction"—tipping off local police to pull someone over for a broken taillight, allowing them to "discover" the evidence legally.
* **The Consequence:** If the players think they have covered their tracks but are suddenly arrested for a minor, unrelated crime, it means the conspiracy has found them and is trying to neutralize them "by the book." However, this is an active battleground. If players can mechanically prove the traffic stop was orchestrated by an intelligence agency, they expose the "Fruit of the Poisonous Tree" and the local charges will collapse under legal scrutiny.
* **Mechanical GM Checks (Parallel Construction in Play):**
  When characters leave digital footprints (e.g., failing a hacking roll, using flagged communications, or neglecting to secure their devices), the GM should not immediately dispatch tactical squads. Instead, the GM rolls a **Parallel Construction Check**:
  * **Dice Pool:** The GM rolls a pool of D10s equal to the group's current **Heat Level + the number of distinct digital infractions/compromises** (maximum pool of 10).
  * **Difficulty Class (DC):** The DC depends on the surveillance infrastructure of the characters' current location:
    * *Dense Panopticon (e.g., smart cities, corporate headquarters, intelligence hubs):* DC 1 (only 1 success needed).
    * *Standard Net (e.g., major cities, transportation terminals):* DC 2.
    * *Loose Net (e.g., rural areas, developing nations):* DC 3.
  * **Resolution Outcomes:**
    * *1 Success:* Local authorities are tipped off via a fabricated anonymous call or automated flag. A patrol unit is dispatched to pull the characters over for a minor pretext (e.g., speeding, broken light) to perform an ID check and search.
    * *2 Successes:* The conspiracy fabricates probable cause. A warrant is generated in the national database, leading to a coordinated police arrest or safehouse raid under standard law enforcement protocols.
    * *3+ Successes:* Complete intelligence integration. Federal or corporate strike teams intercept the characters under the guise of an urgent anti-terrorist or national security operation.


## 4. Trust is a Liability
* **The Rule:** Everyone has a pressure point. A beloved family member, a secret bank account, a hidden addiction. The conspiracy will find it and use it.
* **The Consequence:** Players must constantly compartmentalize information. If an ally doesn't need to know where the safehouse is, don't tell them. If an NPC gets captured, assume everything they know is compromised within 24 hours.

## 5. The Truth Doesn't Matter; Narrative Does
* **The Rule:** Having proof of a crime is useless if the public doesn't believe it. Deepfakes, AI-generated news, and bot farms can instantly discredit any leak.
* **The Consequence:** To "win" a campaign, it's not enough to just steal the encrypted drive. The players must find a way to disseminate the information securely and make it impossible for the media to ignore or spin. They have to hack the narrative, not just the network.

## 6. The Sovereign Void (Jurisdictional Labyrinths)
* **The Rule:** Westphalian national sovereignty is decaying into jurisdictional labyrinths. International waters, offshore tax havens, and Special Economic Zones (SEZs) are not lawless "blank spots"—the law exists, but it is entirely owned by the enemy.
* **The Consequence:** PMCs don't kill you because it's legally empty; they kill you because the ship flies a Bahamian flag of convenience, the PMC is registered to a shell company in the Seychelles, and the local magistrate has been bought off. The void is a legal slaughterhouse engineered through loopholes.

## 7. Systemic Rot and Algorithmic White-Listing
* **The Rule:** The surveillance state is vast, but it is not omnipotent. It suffers from systemic rot. Intelligence agencies refuse to share facial recognition with corporate PMCs due to petty disputes, and cameras have blind spots because city councils embezzled the maintenance budgets.
* **The Consequence:** This is where players survive. Furthermore, the Factions use "Cryptographic White-Listing" to avoid their own systems. PMCs carry tokens that tell city acoustic sensors to ignore their gunfire. Enterprising players can steal or spoof these tokens to buy short windows of consequence-free violence.

## V2.1 Debriefing Optimization Patch

Based on extensive playtesting and simulated feedback, the following mechanical tweaks have been officially implemented to optimize the *Zero Sum* experience:

### 1. Tactical Cover & Evasion
* **The Issue:** Evasion DCs in combat were punishingly high, rapidly draining ammunition tokens and making survivability too low even for prepared characters.
* **The Fix:** **Tactical Cover Bonus.** If a character spends a narrative action to secure physical cover (e.g., behind concrete, server racks, or an armored vehicle) before an attack, their Evasion DC is automatically reduced by 2. Furthermore, they may spend an Analogue Token to guarantee their cover holds for the duration of the firefight.

### 2. Deep Penetration (Multi-Stage Hacking)
* **The Issue:** Hacking felt too simple and binary, lacking the tension appropriate for our zero-trust setting.
* **The Fix:** Hacking is no longer a single check. Bypassing corporate security or secure servers requires a **Deep Penetration Sequence**. The Hacker must achieve 3 cumulative Successes across multiple turns. However, each failed roll immediately generates +1 Metadata Exhaust, escalating the risk of the system tracing their physical location before they finish the hack.

### 3. Ties & The Narrative Override
* **The Issue:** Players felt that their "Ties" (Contacts) were under-utilized and wanted more narrative ways to escape danger or bypass censorship.
* **The Fix:** **The Narrative Override.** Once per session, a player can permanently "burn" one of their Ties to automatically succeed on any non-combat social, investigative, or evasion check (e.g., smuggling a decrypted drive past a checkpoint). Burning a Tie means that contact is compromised, arrested, or goes permanently off-grid to save the player. 

### 4. Streamlined Debt Ledger
* **The Issue:** Calculating exact monetary equivalents for favors was slowing down the game flow at the table.
* **The Fix:** **The Favor Tier System.** The Debt Ledger no longer requires precise dollar amounts. Favors are now strictly tiered:
  - *Minor Favor* (e.g., passing a security checkpoint, a burner phone): Reduces Exhaust by 1.
  - *Major Favor* (e.g., erased flight records, a weapons cache): Reduces Exhaust by 3.
  - *Soul Bound* (e.g., a corporate hit, full identity scrub): Reduces Exhaust by 5, but the GM gains absolute narrative control over the character's next Downtime phase.

### 5. Instant Cognitive Load
* **The Issue:** The triggers for Cognitive Load calculations were occasionally too ambiguous, leading to table debates.
* **The Fix:** **Instant Triggers.** No calculations needed. A character automatically suffers +1 Cognitive Load if they:
  1. Take any physical damage.
  2. Burn a Tie.
  3. Witness the death of an ally or innocent.
  4. Fill their Metadata Exhaust track.
