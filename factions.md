# Factions and Connections: The Web of Influence

In *Zero Sum*, the "monsters" are institutions. They are vast, decentralized, and often work in parallel or in secret conflict with one another. Below are the primary power players in the 2026 landscape.

## 1. The Surveillance State (Intelligence Agencies)
These aren't rogue agents; they are the system working exactly as designed.
* **The "Five Eyes" Apparatus:** A massive, automated dragnet of global communications. In the game, they aren't looking at the players personally until the Heat Level gets too high. 
* **Key Tactics:** Signal interception, freezing assets, "Parallel Construction" (using illegal surveillance to find evidence, then lying about how they found it to use in court).

## 2. Private Intelligence & Cyber Mercenaries
Corporate equivalents of intelligence agencies, offering deniable operations to the highest bidder.
* **Aegis Analytics:** A data-mining firm that weaponizes social media data and psychological profiling to rig elections and destroy reputations.
* **Vanguard Solutions:** Purveyors of military-grade spyware (similar to Pegasus/NSO Group). They sell zero-click smartphone exploits to authoritarian regimes and nervous billionaires.

## 3. High Finance & "Dark Money"

![Dark Money](assets/dark_money_syndicate_redacted.jpg)

The people who actually run the world. They don't use guns; they use algorithmic trading and offshore tax havens.
* **The Sovereign Wealth Syndicates:** Trillions of dollars hidden in shell companies across the Cayman Islands, Delaware, and Swiss Freeports. They will eliminate anyone who threatens their financial secrecy.
* **The "Fixers":** Law firms and wealth managers in London and New York who specialize in hiding assets and legally harassing journalists into silence.

## 4. The Underground & Resistance
The players' potential allies, though none are entirely trustworthy.
* **The Decentralized Web (Hacktivists):** Loose collectives of hackers. They have useful tools but are constantly infiltrated by federal informants.
* **Zero-Day Brokers:** Information merchants who buy and sell software vulnerabilities. They are strictly neutral and incredibly expensive.
* **The Grey Market:** Smugglers, forgers, and ex-military logistics experts who operate outside the traditional banking system, running on cryptocurrency and favors.

## The Web of Connections
In *Zero Sum*, factions constantly overlap. A politician (Faction 1) might hire a Cyber Mercenary (Faction 2) paid for by Dark Money (Faction 3) to discredit a Hacktivist (Faction 4). The GM should always map out at least three degrees of separation between the players and the "Big Boss" of a scenario.
