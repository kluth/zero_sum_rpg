# Analog Session Analysis: Game Design Issues

Based on the review of the 10 analog session transcripts, here are 5 actionable game design issues that need to be fixed:

1. **Bleed Card "Death Spiral" (Loss of Agency)** 
   *Issue:* Bleed Cards frequently incapacitate characters, causing skipped turns (Session 1), deafening (Session 2), blinding (Session 4), or forced PvP/paranoia (Session 3). This removes player agency and creates un-fun death spirals.
   *Actionable Fix:* Redesign Bleed Cards to introduce narrative complications or manageable mechanical trade-offs rather than hard mechanical lockouts.

2. **Action Point (AP) Starvation**
   *Issue:* Players quickly drain their starting pool of 3-5 AP in early phases and are forced to play the climax of the session with 0 AP (Sessions 4, 6, 8, 10). This limits tactical choices and makes late-game resolution overly reliant on luck.
   *Actionable Fix:* Introduce in-game mechanics to recover or generate AP mid-session (e.g., accepting a narrative flaw, taking a breather, or team synergy combos).

3. **Inconsistent Auto-Successes Bypassing Core Mechanics**
   *Issue:* Some GM rulings allow players to spend multiple AP to guarantee an "auto-success" without rolling (Session 1, Session 5). This trivializes the multidimensional dice pool system and encourages rapid AP depletion.
   *Actionable Fix:* Standardize AP usage so it strictly modifies dice pools (adding Advantage/Blue/Green dice) rather than skipping rolls entirely.

4. **Overly Punitive "Threat" (Red Dice) & Brittle Paper Clocks**
   *Issue:* The scaling of Threat and Red Danger dice is too steep. A single high roll on a Red die—even alongside multiple Green Successes—often triggers catastrophic consequences, such as instantly filling multi-segment Paper Clocks to max (Session 9).
   *Actionable Fix:* Re-balance Red die probabilities and mandate that Paper Clocks fill sequentially rather than jumping to full off a single mixed-success roll, preserving tension pacing.

5. **Clunky Analog Syncing (Split-Party Mechanics)**
   *Issue:* Synchronized or simultaneous actions (like the dual-hack in Session 9) require individual perfect rolls; if one player fails, the entire synchronized action is blown catastrophically.
   *Actionable Fix:* Implement a "Group Action" or "Teamwork" mechanic that allows players to pool their dice, average their successes, or spend AP to assist each other during synchronized split-party operations.
